## 1.3 The Three Signatures

![Image](/assets/infographics/TPS_IMG_ThreeSignatures.jpg)

Now that we have audited the Hardware (Input Ports) and the Software (The Process Library), it is time to run a diagnostic on the whole machine.

When you look closely at the data stream, you will notice three specific operational signatures embedded in the code. To the un-hacked mind, these look like bugs or error codes. To a hacker, they are features. They are the keys to the kingdom.

In the Legacy Code, these are known as the *Ti-Lakkhana* (The Three Marks of Existence).

We call them the **Three  Signatures**.

### Signature 1: The Flux (Anicca)

The first thing you notice is the refresh rate.

Nothing stays on the screen. The `kalapas` you read about in the old manuals are not static objects. They are 3D data packets called **Voxels**. They arise and pass away instantly.

This is **Volatility**.

If you look at your hand, it seems solid. That is just the UI fooling you. If you look at the raw data stream of your hand, it is a vibrating mass of sensation. It is blinking in and out of existence at trillions per second (TPS).

* **Legacy Term:** *Anicca* (Impermanence)
* **System Term:** High-Frequency Flux

### Signature 2: The Lag (Dukkha)

This is the friction.

Your brain wants the world to be solid. It wants the "Good Feeling" to last forever. But the Flux (Signature 1) deletes the "Good Feeling" the moment it arises.

The system tries to hold onto a frame that has already been deleted. This causes a rendering error. It generates system heat.

* **Legacy Term:** *Dukkha* (Suffering)
* **System Term:** System Latency

### Signature 3: The Null Pointer (Anatta)

This is the most critical diagnostic.

If the world is just a stream of Voxels, and the body is just a set of Input Ports, who is reading the screen? Who is the User?

In computer science, a "null pointer" occurs when a piece of code tries to point to a memory address that doesn't exist. The program looks for an object called "User," but the address is empty.

The Legacy Code calls this *Anatta*. It translates to "Not-Self."

We call it the **Null Pointer** or **The Empty Admin Folder**.

We naturally assume there is a "Ghost in the Machine." We assume there is a "Me." But when you overclock your attention and look at the refresh rate, you run into a problem. The data is moving too fast for a user to exist.

For a "Self" to exist, it would need to stick around. It would need to be permanent. But the system wipes the cache after every single frame.

When you scan your experience for a solid "Self," you keep getting a "404 Error".

* You look at the body? That is just high-speed haptic buzzing.
* You look at the thoughts? Those are just scrolling text logs.
* You look for the watcher? You just find more watching.

There is no noun. There is only a **verb**.

The "User" is the ultimate bloatware. It is a **Resource Hog** that chews up 90% of your CPU power trying to maintain a history file that doesn't exist.

When you finally see the Null Pointer, when you realize the Admin folder is empty, you don't crash. You fly. The lag disappears.

There is just the Flux. And that is what we call **Root Access**.
