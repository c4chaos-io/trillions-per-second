![Image](/assets/infographics/TPS_IMG_Checkpoint02.jpg)


You think you are running in real-time. You think you are "Live."

You are not.

You are watching a buffered stream.

There is a measurable delay between reality happening and "You" claiming it. Neuroscientists call this the "half-second delay." Sysadmins call it **Network Latency**.

We are going to spot the lag right now.

**The Test:**

1.  Sit comfortably. Close your eyes.
2.  Wait for a sudden sound. A car honk. A door slamming. A bird chirping.
3.  **Watch the sequence.**

**The Sequence:**

1.  **Event:** *POP.* (Raw Sound Data hits the Port).
2.  **Gap:** (Processing...)
3.  **Narrative:** "That was a car." "It was loud." "I didn't like it."

Do you see it?

There is a gap. A tiny split-second of silence before the "I" shows up.

In that first millisecond of the *POP*, there was no "You." There was just hearing.

The "Self" didn't hear the sound. The "Self" is the popup window that appeared *after* the sound to take credit for it.

**The "Self" is a Loading Icon.**

When you see a spinning wheel on a website, you know it isn't the content. It is a placeholder while the system fetches the data.

Your ego is the spinning wheel. It is a post-processing artifact. It is a laggy report filed by the OS to explain what just happened.

**The Hack:**

Stop trying to be faster than the sound. You can't.

Just watch the gap. Notice that "Hearing" happens first, and "Me" happens second.

If "Hearing" happens before "Me," then "Me" cannot be the one doing the hearing.

You aren't the user. You are the lag.
